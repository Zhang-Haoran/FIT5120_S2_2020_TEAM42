A support group is a small group of people with a particular condition, such as depression, anxiety or bipolar disorder, who meet regularly to discuss their experiences, problems and strategies for coping.<\/p>

Some support groups meet in person and some meet online.<\/p><\/div><\/div><\/div><\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t<\/section>\n\t\t\t\t

Research shows that hearing from and sharing with others with similar experiences can be very helpful.<\/p>

A support group can:<\/p>

  • show you that you are not alone<\/li>
  • help you develop new skills in your child in relating to others<\/li>
  • permit you to \u2018open up\u2019 and discuss your situation and feelings in a safe space<\/li>
  • give you practical skills and advice \u2013 such as how to draw up and stick to a treatment plan for your children<\/li>
  • provide new coping strategies you used for your children \u2013 share your solutions and learn from others\u2019 experiences<\/li>
  • strengthen your motivation to stick with a treatment plan for your children.<\/li><\/ul>

    Family members and friends can also benefit from their own support group as they learn more about the mental illness and become more constructively involved in the recovery of their childrens.<\/p><\/div><\/div><\/div><\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t<\/section>\n\t\t\t\t

    Inverloch \u2013 South Gippsland Mental Illness Carers Group<\/div>
    If you are caring for someone with a mental illness, it can be a stressful, lonely and difficult experience. This support group enables people who are caring for someone with a mental illness, that they are not alone, and in the company of other carers where they can find support, friendship, and at times, a laugh! \n\n
    We meet at the Bass Coast Community Health building, next door to the supermarket in Reilly Street, Inverloch, at 10.00am on the 2nd Monday of every month.\n
