Sleep is vital for children\u2019s growth, learning and development. A good night’s sleep promotes both mental and emotional strength. Poor sleeping habits pave way for negative thinking, depression, anxiety and emotional vulnerability since sleep and mental health are closely linked.
Sleeping patterns have the ability to affect how we feel throughout the day. Having a good sleep will help your child be refreshed and they will be able to deal with challenges and be more productive and they will be able to get along with the people around them.<\/p>

During our sleep several important activities take place that help our body in physical recovery and repair, support brain development, cardiac function and body metabolism, along with support learning, improving memory and mood.<\/p><\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

        \u00a0Insomnia<\/b> is characterized by difficulty falling asleep, difficulty staying asleep and\/or early-morning awakenings. Individuals with insomnia feel unrefreshed upon awakening and fatigued during the day. Treatment for insomnia is cognitive-behavioural therapy (CBT) that focuses on thoughts\/worries about sleep and behavioural sleep patterns.<\/span><\/p><\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

Treatment includes reduced intake of caffeine, iron replacement; exercise and stretching and warm baths.<\/span><\/p><\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t
