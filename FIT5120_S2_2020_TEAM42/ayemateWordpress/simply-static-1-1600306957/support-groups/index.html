Support groups

AyeMate > support groups

Find support for your children through others with similar experiences.

A support group is a small group of people with a particular condition, such as depression, anxiety or bipolar disorder, who meet regularly to discuss their experiences, problems and strategies for coping.

Some support groups meet in person and some meet online.

Benefits of support groups

Research shows that hearing from and sharing with others with similar experiences can be very helpful.

A support group can:

  • show you that you are not alone
  • help you develop new skills in your child in relating to others
  • permit you to ‘open up’ and discuss your situation and feelings in a safe space
  • give you practical skills and advice – such as how to draw up and stick to a treatment plan for your children
  • provide new coping strategies you used for your children – share your solutions and learn from others’ experiences
  • strengthen your motivation to stick with a treatment plan for your children.

Family members and friends can also benefit from their own support group as they learn more about the mental illness and become more constructively involved in the recovery of their childrens.
